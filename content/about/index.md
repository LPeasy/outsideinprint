Outside In Print is a digital imprint for essays, dialogues, reported case studies, and working papers published for the web with stable URLs and versioned records.

## Mission

The site exists to publish durable web editions instead of disposable feed posts. Each piece is meant to stand on its own, remain citable, and stay easy to revisit later.

## What the Imprint Publishes

Outside In Print publishes explanatory essays, reported analysis, dialogues, narrative experiments, and editorial dispatches. The archive is organized two ways at once: through curated collections that gather related work into reading threads, and through the full library that exposes the entire published catalog.

## Editorial Model

The site is built around direct publication to the web. Essays carry a publication date, version number, and canonical URL. Collections gather recurring questions, projects, and dossiers without turning the archive into a taxonomy maze.

## Author and Publisher

Outside In Print is the publisher and imprint for the site as a whole. Robert V. Ussley is the default author byline attached to the essay corpus published here.

## How to Read the Site

If you want a quiet introduction, begin at [Home](/) or move through the public [Collections](/collections/). If you want the full catalog, use the [Library](/library/). If you want to enter by chance, follow [Feeling curious?](/random/). If you want the essay corpus under a single byline, use the [author archive](/authors/robert-v-ussley/).
