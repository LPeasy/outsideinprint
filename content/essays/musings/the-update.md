The clock reaches the time you gave someone, and you're across town.

You knew twenty minutes ago that the plan had slipped, but a little voice kept bargaining with the clock. One quick stop. One lucky green light. No need to send the awkward message yet.

Now someone else is sitting beside an empty chair.

Delays happen. Things come up. We let the boat rock with the waves because flat water was never part of the promise. Trouble starts when we keep the old plan alive in our head after the day has moved on.

A short update can't give the lost time back. It can give the waiting person choices again. They can order, leave later, finish something, meet somewhere else, or call the whole thing off. They may be irritated, and you may owe them an apology. The update gives them choices. It doesn't erase the inconvenience.

Fair enough.

The message doesn't need a case for the defense:

“I'm running twenty minutes late. I'm sorry. Start without me if you need to.”

Those words may not make anyone happy. They put both people back in the same moment.

Plans change. An update lets other people change theirs.

## Move With the Water

<figure class="franklin-pullquote" aria-label="Section maxim">
  <blockquote>Let the boat rock. Tell the people riding with you.</blockquote>
  <figcaption>- Robby V.</figcaption>
</figure>

Bold action needs flexibility. A plan gives direction until the ground changes.

A road closes. We turn. A tree falls across the path. Sometimes we can walk around it and save our strength for what can't be avoided.

Acceptance can look weak when stubbornness wears the costume of courage.

Some things do need a direct answer: a hard talk, a broken promise, harm we caused. Flexibility can't be a pretty name for avoidance.

Other bumps need a turn, an honest word, then another step.

We don't have to resist every change because we didn't choose it. We can accept what happened, look at what remains possible, and take the next honest route.

An update says, “This is where I am. This is what I can do now.” It admits the inconvenience and keeps faith with the people inside the plan.

Let the boat rock. When the course changes, call back before someone is left waiting on the old shore.
