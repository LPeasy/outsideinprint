# Minimum Due Source Checklist

## Source Hierarchy

1. Official CFPB consumer guidance on the three-year repayment box and minimum payments.
2. CFPB Regulation Z / 12 CFR 1026.7 periodic statement repayment-disclosure language.
3. CFPB hardship guidance, used only to protect the caveat that some people cannot pay more in a hard month.
4. Research Library search report for duplication control.

## Primary, Official, Direct, Or Public-Record Sources Found

| Source | Type | Role |
|---|---|---|
| CFPB, `A box on my credit card bill says that I will pay off the balance in three years...` | Official consumer guidance | Supports the minimum-payment, more-payment, interest-over-time, and `could take years` claims. |
| CFPB Regulation Z, 12 CFR 1026.7 | Official federal regulation source | Supports the credit-card periodic statement repayment-disclosure claim. |
| CFPB, `What should I do if I can't pay my credit card bills?` | Official consumer guidance | Supports the hardship caveat, not the central proof. |

## Secondary Reporting Used

None. No secondary reporting governs the frame or factual claims.

## Source Gaps

- No issuer-specific minimum-payment formula is used.
- No current APR average, debt-balance average, delinquency trend, or late-fee litigation claim is used.
- No claim is made about why a card issuer sets a particular minimum payment.

## Claims That Must Not Be Made As Certainty

- Every borrower can pay more.
- Minimum payments are a trick, fraud, or deceptive by design.
- Carrying a balance is always a moral failure.
- Paying the minimum always causes years of debt.
- This essay is individualized financial advice.

## Currentness Caveats

CFPB guidance and Regulation Z pages were live-checked on July 9, 2026. Recheck them before any later revision that makes legal, regulatory, APR, debt-balance, delinquency, hardship, or issuer-practice language central.
