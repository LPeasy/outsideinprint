# Source Checklist

Date: 2026-07-05

## Source Hierarchy

1. Official government data: U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, American Time Use Survey 2025 release and chart package.
2. Reputable data analysis: Pew Research Center analysis using ATUS and Census-linked marriage data.
3. Optional academic/background source: cognitive household labor literature, used only if a revision needs a sourced definition of hidden planning work.
4. Prior local research: ResearchLibrary search report and prior Simple Logic package notes, used for workflow and duplication control.

## Primary, Official, Direct, Or Public-Record Sources Found

| Source | Type | Use |
|---|---|---|
| U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, `American Time Use Survey - 2025 Results` | Official government data | Supports household-work baseline, 81 percent daily participation, about 2 hours average, and sex/time-use context. |
| U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics chart, `Average hours per day spent in selected household activities` | Official government data/chart | Supports food preparation, cleaning, laundry, household management, and other household-activity categories. |
| Pew Research Center, `In a Growing Share of U.S. Marriages, Husbands and Wives Earn About the Same` | Reputable secondary data analysis | Supports context that paid earning balance can coexist with uneven home work. |

## Secondary Reporting Used

- Pew is used as attributed analysis, not as proof of motive or a universal rule.
- No major-media news framing is used.

## Source Gaps

- No source directly measures "default owner" as a national category in the essay's exact terms.
- No source proves why any specific person fails to notice, plan, or do a task.
- No legal source supports household-duty claims; none are made.

## Claims That Must Not Be Made As Certainty

- Do not claim one sex, spouse, roommate, or family member is always the default owner.
- Do not claim unassigned chores always become resentment.
- Do not claim "make me a list" is always bad. It can help when ownership follows the list.
- Do not claim national time-use averages prove a specific household's moral fault.

## Currentness Caveats

- BLS ATUS 2025 was released June 25, 2026 and checked live on July 5, 2026.
- Pew 2023 analysis is still useful for context, but any stronger current trend claim would need newer data.
- Before publication, recheck BLS ATUS pages and any Pew updates if the deck or promo copy uses exact figures.
