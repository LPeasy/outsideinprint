# Source Checklist

Title: `The Examiner's Red Pencil`
Date: `2026-05-26`

## Source Hierarchy

1. Primary and official records: Federal Reserve Board statements, speeches, supervisory operating principles, banking-agency notice, Federal Register notice, capital-rule materials, and Fed SVB review.
2. Official institutional claims: Fed and agency releases are used as statements of agency position and process, not neutral fact.
3. Secondary reporting: Reuters is used only as an attributed current-reporting lead for bank lobbying and stakeholder pressure.
4. Advocacy or industry claims: Sought through current reporting and public comment process; no direct bank trade comment was treated as governing evidence.

## Primary Sources Sought But Unavailable or Not Used

- Direct public comments from large-bank trade groups on the May 2026 CAMELS notice were sought but the comment window remains open and a complete docket record was not treated as available for final claims.
- Internal bank lobbying documents described by Reuters are not public. The essay avoids asserting their content beyond Reuters' attributed report.
- Current Fed examiner manuals implementing the April 2026 operating principles may evolve. The essay relies on the public operating-principles statement and direct speeches.

## Assumptions Rejected

- Rejected the assumption that bank complaints about MRAs are automatically self-interested evasions.
- Rejected the assumption that examiner discretion is automatically neutral public protection.
- Rejected the assumption that lighter supervision equals sound reform.
- Rejected the assumption that stricter supervision equals safety without regard to clarity and evidence.
- Rejected the Reuters win/loss lobbying frame as the essay's structure.

## Source Risk

`LOW-MEDIUM`

The core public policy records are strong. The current lobbying detail is secondary and attributed. The essay's thesis does not depend on proving the full private lobbying record.
