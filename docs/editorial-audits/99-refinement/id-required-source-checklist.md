# Source Checklist

Essay: `ID Required`

Decision: `SOURCE_RECORD_READY`

## Media Frame Quarantine

- Inherited media frame: AP and related coverage emphasize purge risk; Reuters/ProPublica/Texas Tribune emphasize legal and administrative error risk.
- Official frame also quarantined: federal integrity language presents verification as self-validating.
- Rejected assumption: voter ID and voter-roll verification are illegitimate until access advocates accept them.
- Rejected counter-assumption: official integrity language proves accuracy, lawful authority, or sound process.
- Revised OIP frame: voting is a civic franchise with responsibilities attached; identification and verification are legitimate when tied to law, evidence, state authority, county procedure, citizen notice, proof, correction, and audit.

## Claims Checked

- EO 14399 established a State Citizenship List and assigned DHS/USCIS/SSA roles: supported by EO sources.
- EO 14399 says appearance on the State Citizenship List does not itself register anyone to vote: supported by EO sources.
- FDA rules require photo ID for regulated pseudoephedrine purchases: supported by FDA guidance.
- NCSL counted 36 states requesting or requiring voter identification at the polls: supported by NCSL.
- SAVE supplies verification information and does not itself decide voter eligibility: supported by USCIS fact sheet.
- NVRA list maintenance requires reasonable state efforts and imposes procedural limits: supported by DOJ/NVRA sources.
- DOJ sued five additional states and said the total reached 29 states and DC: supported by DOJ February 26 release.
- Fontes dismissed the Arizona case and rejected DOJ's production-authority theory in that case: supported by court order.
- AP and ProPublica/Texas Tribune examples remain attributed secondary reporting, not national error-rate proof.
