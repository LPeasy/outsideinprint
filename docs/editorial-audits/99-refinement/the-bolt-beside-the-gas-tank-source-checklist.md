## Claim Check

- Program targets, compressed schedule, tank location, 20- and 21-mile-per-hour tests, alternative configurations, and the proposed buffer trace to the Harris textbook and the supplied case analysis. The essay links the public textbook record at the first use of that evidence; the student PDF remains audit-only.
- The draft preserves the supplied analysis's caution that the alternative tests may not have been directly comparable and that production feasibility remains unresolved.
- The paper applies an $11-per-vehicle input across 12.5 million vehicles and reports a $137 million modeled cost, although the listed inputs multiply to $137.5 million. It reports benefits of $49.15 million, although the listed components total $49.53 million. The essay preserves the reported figures and states both arithmetic discrepancies.
- The essay removes the supplied analysis's 1973 memo date. The Alexander Street catalog linked from MIT OpenCourseWare dates the Ford paper to 1977, which is enough to establish the narrower point that it followed the 1967-1970 design period.
- The essay states that the supplied materials do not connect the $11 assumption to the proposed Pinto buffer.
- The 1978 defect description, Ford's disagreement, 1.4 million vehicle estimate, station-wagon exclusion, principal remedy components, and the 1977-model rear-impact requirement trace to the NHTSA recall file.
- The organizational-risk and professional-duty claims are the author's analysis and are stated as judgment.
- The essay does not assign knowledge, motive, or authority to every Ford engineer or executive.
- The essay does not offer an aggregate death or injury total because the narrow record used for this adaptation does not establish one reliably.

## Media-Frame Quarantine

- Inherited frame: Ford knowingly chose to burn customers because an $11 repair cost more than expected victim payments.
- Assumed burden rejected: one later fleetwide memo proves the motive for the original Pinto design.
- Primary-source rebuild: NHTSA recall documents establish the later federal defect determination, affected vehicles, failure modes, remedy, and regulatory chronology.
- Secondary-source role: the supplied Harris-based case analysis governs the earlier program and test reconstruction; MIT supplies memo provenance; OECD supplies a current technical definition.
- Assumptions rejected before drafting: that $11 priced a specific Pinto repair; that every alternative test was directly comparable; that the later memo governed the 1967-1970 design; and that job title alone establishes personal responsibility.
- Source hierarchy: federal recall record; authenticated course textbook and supplied technical case record; memo bibliographic catalog; official OECD analysis; explanatory sources.

## Source Gaps
