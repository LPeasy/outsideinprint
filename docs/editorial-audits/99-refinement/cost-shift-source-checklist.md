# Source Checklist

## Source Hierarchy

1. Official executive source: White House Ratepayer Protection Pledge.
2. Official agency sources: Department of Energy data-center energy-use report announcement and EIA electricity price factors explainer.
3. Official regulator source: FERC June 18, 2026 large-load action.
4. Secondary reporting: AP, FT, and trade outlets used only as discovery leads.
5. Advocacy, corporate, and campaign claims: not used as proof.

## Secondary Reporting Used Only As Lead Material

- AP July 2, 2026 live and policy leads helped identify alternative candidate premises.
- FT July 2, 2026 lead helped confirm live attention to FERC data-center action, but the essay relies on FERC records instead.
- Trade coverage on AI data centers and ratepayer costs was not used as factual proof.

## Source Gaps

- No final tariff filings from the six regional grid operators were reviewed in this pass.
- No state rate-case order was used to prove actual local bill effects.
- No independent verified bill dataset was used to claim household savings or cost increases.

## Claims That Must Not Be Made As Certainty

- The pledge has already lowered household electricity costs.
- AI companies will pay every future data-center cost in every region.
- Data centers are the only or main national cause of rising electric bills.
- FERC has already solved large-load cost shifting.
- The Ohio project proves the national policy works.
- AI demand will inevitably raise all household bills.

## Key Caveat For Revised Copy

Do not claim AI demand inevitably raises all household bills. The supported claim is narrower: rising data-center electricity demand creates upward price pressure when usable supply, transmission, fuel, and reliability capacity do not keep pace.

## Currentness Caveats

Access date: 2026-07-03.
FERC proceedings, demand projections, and electricity-market conditions may change quickly. Recheck official dockets, tariff filings, EIA records, and state utility records before publication or later reuse.
