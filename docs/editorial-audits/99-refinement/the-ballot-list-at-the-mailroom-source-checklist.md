## Media-Frame Quarantine

Inherited frame: AP and downstream coverage frame the story as a federal judge allowing Trump's voter-list and mail-voting order to move forward, with Democrats and civil-rights groups warning of chaos and disenfranchisement.

Assumed moral burden: The coverage tends to make the administration justify access burdens while treating plaintiffs as the natural defenders of voters. Opposite partisan coverage tends to make challengers justify any resistance to fraud prevention. Both frames are incomplete.

Assumptions rejected:

- Citizenship verification is suspect by default.
- Federal data matching is reliable by design.
- A court denial of preliminary relief means the order is lawful in final form.
- Future harm can be ignored because it is future.
- Every mail-ballot rule change is suppression.
- Every election-integrity claim is self-proving.

Final source discipline: Legal and official records govern the essay; AP stays as live-news lead; advocacy claims and partisan readings are quarantined.
