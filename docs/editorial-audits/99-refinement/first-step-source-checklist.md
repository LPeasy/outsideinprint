# First Step Source Checklist

## Source Hierarchy

1. CDC official adult physical-activity guidance.
2. CDC official getting-started guidance.
3. HHS Office of Disease Prevention and Health Promotion guideline explanation.
4. Research Library search report for prior-work and duplication control.

## Primary, Official, Direct, Or Public-Record Sources Found

| Source | Type | Role |
|---|---|---|
| CDC, [Adult Activity: An Overview](https://www.cdc.gov/physical-activity-basics/guidelines/adults.html) | Official public-health guidance | Supports the weekly 150-minute target, two muscle-strengthening days, smaller chunks, and `some physical activity is better than none`. |
| CDC, [Tips for Getting More Physically Active Minutes](https://www.cdc.gov/physical-activity/features/getting-more-active-minutes.html) | Official public-health guidance | Supports starting slowly, adding minutes, and working toward the weekly target. |
| HHS Office of Disease Prevention and Health Promotion, [Physical Activity Guidelines Questions & Answers](https://odphp.health.gov/our-work/nutrition-physical-activity/physical-activity-guidelines/about-physical-activity-guidelines/questions-answers) | Official federal guideline explanation | Supports starting with small amounts and working up toward the guideline over time for people who are inactive. |

## Secondary Reporting Used

None. No secondary reporting governs the frame or factual claims.

## Source Gaps

- No prevalence claim says how many people treat the target as a gate.
- No clinical claim is made for a specific person, diagnosis, injury, disability, medication, or exercise type.
- No weight-loss, disease-treatment, mood-treatment, or longevity claim is needed for the argument.

## Claims That Must Not Be Made As Certainty

- Any one first step is safe for everyone.
- A short session guarantees a health result.
- People who miss the target lack discipline.
- The guideline itself causes all-or-nothing thinking.

## Currentness Caveats

CDC adult guidance, CDC starting guidance, and HHS/ODPHP guidance were live-checked on July 10, 2026. Recheck them before any later revision that makes clinical recommendations, disability guidance, or specific activity examples more central.
