## Source Hierarchy

1. Direct historical and religious texts for what those texts teach.
2. Original research record for a bounded association about goal adjustment.
3. Official USDA material for the concrete fact that farm production faces conditions outside a grower's full command.
4. No secondary reporting is needed to supply the central argument.

## Primary, Official, And Direct Sources Found

- [Epictetus, *Enchiridion* 1](https://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Perseus%3Atext%3A1999.01.0236%3Atext%3Denc) ‾ direct historical text; it distinguishes one's acts from external things.
- [*Bhagavad Gita* 2.47](https://www.gitasupersite.iitk.ac.in/srimad?etadi=1&etgb=1&etpurohit=1&etsiva=1&etssa=1&field_chapter_value=2&field_nsutra_value=47&language=dv) ‾ direct religious text; used only as a related teaching about action and result.
- [Wrosch et al., 2003](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15018681/) ‾ original study record; three studies reported associations between goal adjustment capacities and subjective well-being.
- [USDA ERS, Risk in Agriculture](https://www.ers.usda.gov/topics/farm-practices-management/risk-management/risk-in-agriculture) ‾ official source; weather, disease, pests, prices, and other factors affect farm production and income.

## Secondary Sources

None used in the draft. Search results and source summaries served as lead material only.

## Source Gaps

- No source can establish a universal law that detachment leads to success or peace.
- The philosophical texts have translation and interpretive differences.
- The goal-adjustment study reports associations in particular samples; it does not provide individualized advice or prove a causal benefit.
- No cited source establishes that stronger attachment makes desires slip away, that resistance to the universe only causes frustration and heartache, or that spiritual surrender guarantees an emotional outcome. The published body identifies those passages as personal faith and metaphor rather than empirical proof.
- The cited *Bhagavad Gita* and Epictetus source do not establish a Christian claim about God, and `energy circulating in your own body` is not a supported physiological claim.

## Claims That Must Not Be Made As Certainty

- Harder attachment always makes a person fail.
- Release always improves a person's health, mood, income, relationships, or outcome.
- Any faith tradition proves a universal causal rule.
- A farmer's effort is irrelevant to a harvest.
- A spiritual maxim is an empirical claim about frustration, heartache, or the future.
- A reader should treat the user-directed God and body-energy language as medical, psychological, or cross-tradition proof.

## Currentness Caveats

This is a durable premise, not a current-event essay. USDA's factual agricultural-risk page and the Gita source page were checked live on 2026-07-13. Retain the distinction between a source's teaching, a study's association, the essay's practical inference, and the disclosed personal-faith reflection.
