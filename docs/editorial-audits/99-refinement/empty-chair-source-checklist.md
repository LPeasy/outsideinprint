# Source Checklist

Date: 2026-07-03  
Package: `Empty Chair`

## Source Hierarchy

1. Official federal data and advisories.
2. Direct research report from AARP/National Alliance for Caregiving.
3. Health-system and mental-health nonprofit guidance for boundary fair-version support.
4. Prior local OIP Research Library entries for editorial method only.
5. Secondary reporting not used as evidence.

## Secondary Reporting Used

None for factual claims. Search results from media pages were not used.

## Source Gaps

- No nationally representative source was found or needed for `boundary language` as a cultural trend.
- No legal research was performed on family-support duties by state.
- No causal evidence was reviewed linking boundary rhetoric to caregiving shortfalls.
- No source measures how often an individual boundary shifts care to another person; the essay treats that as a conditional logic claim.

## Claims That Must Not Be Made As Certainty

- Boundary culture caused the caregiving crisis.
- A specific family member has a legal duty in ordinary care situations.
- A person must remain in dangerous or abusive relationships.
- One gender, age cohort, sibling position, ideology, or class is chiefly to blame.
- AARP/NAC and BLS counts are directly comparable or additive.

## Currentness Caveats

- BLS eldercare release is for 2023-24 and was released September 25, 2025.
- AARP/NAC caregiving report was published July 24, 2025.
- HHS/SAMHSA/UC Davis/MHA materials should be rechecked before publication if the essay is revised after 2026.
