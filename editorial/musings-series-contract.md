# Musings Series Contract

Musings is Outside In Print's short-form source-free reflection lane. It holds brief personal observations, questions, recognitions, and invitations to attention. It is not an essay, report, research note, or public-claim package.

## What Belongs Here

- First-person reflection and ordinary lived experience.
- A single thought, image, question, or small practical recognition.
- Short blurbs with no fixed word count, no required section heading, and no required visual.
- Plain language that leaves room for the reader instead of trying to prove a large conclusion.

## Source-Free Boundary

Musings never require sources. They are limited to reflection that makes no external factual claim, so no citation, source checklist, research package, or OIP-99 report is required in this lane.

This boundary does not turn an assertion into a fact. Do not use Musings for empirical, historical, current, legal, medical, statistical, psychological, spiritual-mechanism, or named-actor claims. If a sentence needs a source, it belongs in another OIP lane; recast it as clearly personal reflection or move it there.

Do not add a named source merely to decorate a personal reflection. Do not promise a reader a result, diagnose them, or present a private feeling as universal proof. A site integration may carry a compact internal source-free editorial record when it needs one, but that record must not add sources or external evidence claims to the Musing.

## Authoring And Publication

New entries belong under content/essays/musings/ so they remain in the archive and Library. Use explicit front matter with a title, date, draft state, concise public description, section_label: "Musing", library_type: "musing", and collections: ["musings"].

The source-free exception requires both source_mode: "SOURCE_FREE" and external_factual_claims: "none". A public Musing needs either a social image or a documented image_exempt reason, but no visual is otherwise required.

Musings are exempt from the essay-specific OIP-99 package only while all of those source-free declarations are accurate. Standard site, accessibility, and house-style checks remain in force. If an external factual claim becomes central, stop treating the piece as a Musing and reopen the appropriate evidence and editorial controls.
