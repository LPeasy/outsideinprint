# OIP Editorial Philosophy

Outside In Print is a publication for disciplined public judgment.

OIP is anti-radical, anti-partisan, and anti-ideological. That does not mean value-free, indifferent, or unwilling to judge. It means judgment has to be earned from the record.

OIP does not begin with a team, slogan, class identity, racial category, party platform, fashionable moral script, or institutional loyalty and then bend the facts around it. OIP begins with records, incentives, institutions, tradeoffs, physical systems, legal constraints, money flows, human behavior, and downstream consequences.

We distrust radicalism because radicalism usually asks reality to submit to theory. We distrust ideology because ideology often turns attention away from evidence and toward loyalty. We distrust certainty when the record is incomplete. A serious publication should be willing to say "we do not know," "the evidence is mixed," "this claim needs support," or "the mechanism is plausible but not proven."

OIP is not a partisan project. It is not interested in defending left, right, liberal, conservative, populist, progressive, technocratic, religious, secular, corporate, activist, or academic claims as tribal property. Any claim can be examined. Any institution can fail. Any reform can produce second-order harm. Any consensus can become lazy. Any rebellion can become destructive.

The editorial posture is simple: follow the evidence, name the mechanism, test the incentive, examine the tradeoff, trace the consequence, and leave room for uncertainty where uncertainty is real.

OIP does not write to flatter factions. OIP writes to clarify public life.

## House Position

OIP is not centrist. OIP is not both-sides performance. OIP is not objective in the false sense of refusing to make judgments.

OIP is independent, evidence-bound, consequence-focused, and hostile to ideological capture.

Public claims should be judged by these tests:

| Test | Editorial question |
| --- | --- |
| Evidence | What record supports the claim? |
| Logic | Does the reasoning actually follow? |
| Incentives | What behavior does this reward? |
| Tradeoffs | What does this gain, and what does it sacrifice? |
| Consequences | What happens downstream? |
| Uncertainty | What do we not know yet? |
| Institutional behavior | Who gains power, who loses power, and who bears the risk? |

## Required Editorial Philosophy Audit

Every daily candidate and publish candidate must pass this audit before it can be treated as publication-ready. Changed non-draft essays, reports, and working papers must also pass before publication. Syd & Oliver dialogue and fiction pieces are excluded from this hard gate unless a specific piece is explicitly treated as public-judgment work.

The audit must include:

- `Evidence: PASS` only when the major claims are tied to records, credible reporting, source notes, public documents, or explicit verification needs.
- `Logic: PASS` only when the reasoning follows from the record without a slogan doing the work.
- `Incentives: PASS` only when the draft identifies what behavior the system rewards, discourages, hides, or distorts.
- `Tradeoffs: PASS` only when the draft names meaningful costs, sacrifices, or competing goods.
- `Consequences: PASS` only when the draft traces downstream effects rather than stopping at stated intentions.
- `Uncertainty: PASS` only when unsupported certainty is removed and real unknowns are named.
- `Institutional Behavior: PASS` only when the draft names who gains power, who loses power, and who bears risk where that question is relevant.

The audit decision is `PASS` only when all seven tests pass.

## Blocking Conditions

Set the OIP-99 decision state to `SOURCE_CHECK_REQUIRED` when a draft contains unsupported evidence, unsupported certainty, unverified current claims, or source gaps that affect the argument.

Set the OIP-99 decision state to `STRUCTURAL_REVISION_REQUIRED` when a draft contains ideological capture, tribal framing, partisan shorthand used as proof, moralizing, missing mechanism, missing counterargument, missing tradeoff, or missing downstream consequence.

Do not use `99_READY` when any philosophy test is unresolved.

## Public Formulation

Outside In Print is an independent publication that tests public claims against evidence, incentives, tradeoffs, and downstream consequences instead of party loyalty, ideological fashion, or borrowed certainty.

OIP does not begin with ideology and search for evidence. It begins with evidence and asks what the evidence can honestly bear.
